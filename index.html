About

I hold a B.Sc. in Computer Science from Ashesi University, and that foundation instilled something in me early: a conviction that software should be principled, not just functional.

That conviction followed me to the United States, where I completed an M.S. in Cybersecurity at Eastern Illinois University. Since then I've been building at EIU, writing APIs, integrating enterprise systems, deploying infrastructure, and quietly making 54 campus departments run better than they did before.

Over 8+ years across Ghana, the US, and remote projects, I've worked across the full stack, but the backend is where I'm most at home. I think in systems: clean architecture, domain-driven design, security-first API patterns. I believe good software is mostly invisible, because it just works.

Right now I'm looking for a team that cares about doing things right, a place where engineering principles aren't just buzzwords on a job listing, and where I can grow alongside people who push each other.
